4. Phase 1: Environmental Diagnosis (SWOT)

The SWOT module helps you systematically analyze your organization's Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats.

Manual SWOT Entry

  1. Navigate to Diagnostics → SWOT Analysis
  2. You'll see four quadrants:
    • Top Left (Green): Internal Strengths
    • Top Right (Red): Internal Weaknesses
    • Bottom Left (Blue): External Opportunities
    • Bottom Right (Orange): External Threats
  3. To add an item:
    • Click "+ Add Item" in the appropriate quadrant
    • Enter a clear, specific statement (e.g., "Strong brand recognition in Southeast Asian markets")
    • Provide Evidence (optional but recommended): Supporting data or source (e.g., "Brand tracking study Q1 2025")
    • Set Impact Level: High, Medium, or Low
    • Click "Save"
Best Practice: Be specific rather than generic. Instead of "Good team," write "Experienced data science team with 15+ years combined industry experience."

AI-Assisted SWOT Generation

To accelerate your analysis using AI:

  1. Click the "AI Assist" button in the SWOT header
  2. Provide context in the text box:
    • Describe your organization, market position, and current challenges
    • Include relevant industry trends or recent changes
    • Paste recent annual reports, survey results, or meeting notes
  3. Select which quadrants to generate (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
  4. Click "Generate SWOT Analysis"
  5. Review AI-generated items:
    • Click "Add to Plan" to include suggested items
    • Click "Edit" to modify before adding
    • Click "Discard" to reject suggestions
⚠️ Important:

AI suggestions are starting points. Always review for accuracy and add specific evidence before finalizing.

Managing SWOT Items

  • Edit: Click the pencil icon on any item to modify text, evidence, or impact
  • Delete: Click the trash icon to remove (items are soft-deleted and can be restored by administrators)
  • Comment: Click the comment bubble to start a discussion thread about specific SWOT items
  • Filter: Use the filter dropdown to show only High-impact items or specific categories

5. Phase 2: Systems Thinking

This advanced module helps you visualize complex relationships between variables in your strategic environment.

Causal Loop Diagrams (CLDs)

CLDs show how different factors influence each other through feedback loops.

Creating a Diagram:

  1. Go to Diagnostics → Systems Thinking
  2. Click "New Diagram"
  3. Add Nodes (variables):
    • Double-click on the canvas to create a node
    • Label it clearly (e.g., "Employee Training," "Customer Satisfaction," "Revenue")
    • Categorize: Strength, Weakness, Opportunity, Threat, or Neutral
  4. Create Links (relationships):
    • Click and drag from one node to another
    • Indicate relationship type:
      • Positive (+): If A increases, B increases (same direction)
      • Negative (-): If A increases, B decreases (opposite direction)
    • Add delay indicators if the effect takes time to manifest
  5. Identify Loops:
    • The system automatically highlights feedback loops
    • Reinforcing Loops (R): Vicious or virtuous cycles that amplify change (marked with "R")
    • Balancing Loops (B): Stabilizing mechanisms that resist change (marked with "B")

Systems Archetypes (Templates)

Apply pre-built system archetypes to common strategic challenges:

  1. Click "Apply Archetype"
  2. Select from five patterns:
    • Limits to Growth: Rapid growth hits constraints
    • Shifting the Burden: Symptom solutions undermine fundamental fixes
    • Escalation: Competitive dynamics spiral out of control
    • Fixes that Fail: Quick fixes create unintended consequences
    • Tragedy of the Commons: Individual optimization harms collective resources
  3. The template loads a starter diagram relevant to your situation
  4. Customize nodes and links to match your specific context
Use Case: If you're experiencing rapid growth but sense impending constraints, apply "Limits to Growth" to identify where bottlenecks will emerge.

6. Phase 3: Strategy Formulation

The Strategy Matrix translates your SWOT analysis into actionable strategic initiatives using the TOWS methodology.

Understanding the Strategy Matrix

Opportunities (External) Threats (External)
Strengths (Internal) SO Strategies
(Maxi-Maxi):
Use strengths to seize opportunities		 ST Strategies
(Maxi-Mini):
Use strengths to avoid threats
Weaknesses (Internal) WO Strategies
(Mini-Maxi):
Overcome weaknesses by exploiting opportunities		 WT Strategies
(Mini-Mini):
Minimize weaknesses and avoid threats

Building Your Strategy Matrix

  1. Navigate to Strategy → Strategy Matrix
  2. You'll see four quadrants corresponding to SO, ST, WO, and WT strategies
  3. Click "+ Add Strategy" in the appropriate quadrant
  4. Write a clear strategy statement that links specific SWOT elements:
    • Example SO Strategy: "Leverage our strong brand recognition (Strength) to capture government digitalization contracts (Opportunity) through targeted public sector marketing"
    • Example WT Strategy: "Address skills gap in data analytics (Weakness) by avoiding markets requiring heavy AI investment (Threat) and focusing on traditional service lines"
  5. Set Priority: High (critical), Medium (important), or Low (nice-to-have)
  6. Link to specific SWOT items using the dropdown to maintain traceability

AI Strategy Generation

To generate strategic options automatically:

  1. Click "AI Generate Strategies"
  2. Select your strategic focus area (e.g., "Market Expansion," "Digital Transformation," "Cost Optimization")
  3. Choose time horizon: Short-term (1 year), Medium-term (2-3 years), or Long-term (5+ years)
  4. Click "Generate Options"
  5. Review AI suggestions and click "Add to Matrix" for viable options

Strategy Management

  • Drag and Drop: Reorder strategies within quadrants by priority
  • Link to SWOT: Ensure each strategy references specific SWOT items (shows traceability icons)
  • Status Tracking: Mark strategies as Proposed, Approved, or Deferred
  • Assign Owners: Designate strategy champions responsible for implementation

7. Phase 4: Balanced Scorecard

Translate strategies into measurable Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) across four perspectives.

The Four Perspectives

  1. Financial: How do we look to shareholders? (Revenue, costs, ROI)
  2. Customer: How do customers see us? (Satisfaction, retention, market share)
  3. Internal Process: What must we excel at? (Efficiency, quality, cycle time)
  4. Learning & Growth: How can we sustain improvement? (Skills, innovation, culture)

Creating KPIs

  1. Go to Scorecard → Balanced Scorecard
  2. Select a perspective tab (Financial, Customer, Internal, Learning)
  3. Click "+ Add KPI"
  4. Define the KPI:
    • KPI Name: Clear metric (e.g., "Revenue Growth," "Customer Satisfaction Score")
    • Target: Goal value (e.g., "15%" or "4.5/5")
    • Unit: %, ₱, days, score, count, etc.
    • Weight: Importance relative to other KPIs (0.0 to 1.0, total should equal 1.0 per perspective)
    • Current Actual: Current performance value
    • Status: On Track, At Risk, Behind, or Complete
  5. Click "Save KPI"

KPI Status & Alerts

The system automatically tracks performance:

  • 🟢 On Track Meeting or exceeding target
  • 🟡 At Risk Within 10% of missing target (triggers email alert to plan owner)
  • 🔴 Behind Missing target by >10% (triggers email alert)
  • ✅ Complete Target achieved

Updating Progress:

  1. Click on any KPI to open the edit panel
  2. Update the "Actual" value
  3. Status updates automatically based on target comparison
  4. Add notes explaining the variance (e.g., "Q2 marketing campaign exceeded expectations")

Scorecard Visualization

  • Perspective Dashboard: See all KPIs color-coded by status
  • Strategy Mapping: Visual lines connect KPIs to the strategies they support
  • Trend Charts: Historical performance graphs (if updated regularly)

8. Phase 5: Initiatives & Execution

Break strategies down into concrete Programs, Activities, and Projects (PAP) with budget tracking.

Understanding the Hierarchy

  • Programs: Large-scale, long-term initiatives comprising multiple projects (e.g., "Digital Transformation Program")
  • Projects: Discrete efforts with specific deliverables (e.g., "CRM Implementation")
  • Activities: Specific tasks or events within projects (e.g., "Staff Training Workshop")

Creating Initiatives

  1. Navigate to Execution → Initiatives
  2. Click "+ New Initiative"
  3. Complete the details:
    • Name: Clear, action-oriented title
    • Type: Program, Project, or Activity
    • Owner: Person or department responsible
    • Budget: Total allocated amount (₱)
    • Utilized: Amount spent to date (₱)
    • Progress: Percentage complete (0-100%)
    • Status: On Track, At Risk, Behind, or Complete
    • Due Date: Target completion date
  4. Link to Strategy: Select which strategy(ies) this initiative supports from the dropdown
  5. Click "Save Initiative"

Budget Tracking

The system automatically calculates:

  • Total Budget: Sum of all initiative budgets
  • Total Utilized: Sum of all spending
  • Remaining: Budget minus utilized
  • Burn Rate: Visual indicators if spending outpaces progress

Updating Progress:

  • Edit initiatives regularly to update % complete and budget utilized
  • Upload supporting documents (invoices, completion reports) to the comments section
  • Mark milestones complete to auto-update progress percentages

Gantt-Style Timeline

Switch to Timeline View to see:

  • Initiative start and end dates
  • Dependencies between projects
  • Critical path visualization
  • Overlapping resource allocations

9. Collaboration & Team Management

Strat Planner Pro is built for teams. Work simultaneously with colleagues anywhere in the world.

Sharing Your Plan

  1. Open the plan you want to share
  2. Click the "Share" button (top right)
  3. Enter colleague's email address
  4. Set permission level:
    • Viewer: Can view all sections but cannot edit (good for board members, external consultants)
    • Editor: Can add and edit content but cannot delete the plan or manage sharing
    • Admin: Full control including deleting items and managing team access
  5. Add an optional message explaining the plan context
  6. Click "Send Invitation"
  7. The recipient receives an email with a link to access the plan
📝 Note:

Recipients must have a Strat Planner Pro account (free) to access shared plans.

Real-Time Collaboration Features

When multiple people work on the same plan:

  • Live Presence: See colored avatars in the top bar showing who's online
  • Live Cursors: See where others are working (when in same section)
  • Instant Updates: Changes appear immediately on everyone's screen
  • Conflict Prevention: The system prevents two people from editing the exact same text field simultaneously

Commenting & Discussion

Add context and questions without cluttering the content:

  1. On any SWOT item, KPI, or Initiative, click the speech bubble icon
  2. Type your comment or question
  3. Click "Post Comment"
  4. Tagged users receive email notifications
  5. Resolve Comments: When the discussion concludes, click "Resolve" to archive the thread (resolved comments remain visible in the history)

@Mentions: Type @ followed by a team member's name to send them a specific notification.

Organization Management

For enterprise users:

Creating an Organization:

  1. Go to Profile → Organizations
  2. Click "Create Organization"
  3. Enter organization name, type (Corporation, Government, NGO, Education), and description
  4. You become the Organization Admin automatically

Inviting Team Members:

  1. In your organization settings, click "Invite Members"
  2. Enter email addresses (bulk paste supported)
  3. Set default role (Viewer, Editor, or Admin)
  4. Set expiration (invitations expire in 7 days for security)
  5. Send invitations

Organization vs. Plan Permissions:

  • Organization Membership: Gives access to the organization's template library and shared resources
  • Plan Sharing: Specific access to individual strategic plans (a user can be in your organization but only have access to specific plans)

13. Frequently Asked Questions

General Usage

Q: Can I use Strat Planner Pro on my mobile device?

A: Yes. Access the web app through your mobile browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge). The interface is fully responsive. For best results on tablets, use landscape mode when editing complex diagrams.

Q: Is my data secure?

A: Yes. All connections use HTTPS encryption. Your password is hashed using industry-standard bcrypt encryption. Data is stored in secure databases with regular automated backups.

Q: What happens if I lose internet connection while working?

A: The app is designed to work offline. Your changes are saved locally in your browser. When you reconnect, changes automatically sync to the server. A banner will indicate "Offline Mode" when disconnected.

Q: Can I export my data if I stop using the service?

A: Yes. Go to Plan Settings → Export Data to download your complete plan in JSON format, which can be imported into other systems or saved for records.

Collaboration

Q: How many people can collaborate on one plan?

A: There is no technical limit, but for optimal performance, we recommend maximum 20 simultaneous editors per plan. Unlimited viewers are supported.

Q: Can I restrict someone from seeing financial data but let them edit SWOT?

A: Currently, permission levels (Viewer/Editor/Admin) apply to the entire plan. For sensitive financial data, consider creating a separate "Financial Annex" plan with restricted sharing.

Q: What if two people edit the same item simultaneously?

A: The system uses "last write wins" for simplicity. If conflicts occur frequently in your team, we recommend using the commenting feature to coordinate who is editing which section.

Technical Issues

Q: The page isn't loading or is showing an error.

A: Try these steps in order:

  1. Refresh the browser (F5 or Ctrl+R / Cmd+R)
  2. Clear browser cache and cookies for strategy-ai-planner.deploypad.app
  3. Log out and log back in
  4. Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari)
  5. Contact support if issues persist

Q: I didn't receive my invitation email.

A: Check your spam/junk folder first. If not found, ensure the email address was typed correctly. Invitations expire after 7 days; ask the sender to resend if expired.

Q: How do I reset my password?

A: On the login page, click "Forgot Password." Enter your email. You'll receive a link valid for 1 hour to create a new password. For security, this logs you out of all devices.

Billing & Accounts

Q: Is there a cost to use Strat Planner Pro?

A: Basic accounts are free for up to 3 plans. Organization accounts with unlimited plans and advanced features require a subscription. Contact sales@deploypad.app for pricing.

Q: How do I delete my account?

A: Go to Profile Settings → Advanced → Delete Account. This permanently removes your user data but preserves plans you own (ownership transfers to organization admin) or plans shared with you (removes your access).

Q: Can I change my email address?

A: Yes, but you must verify the new email address before the change takes effect. Go to Profile Settings → Change Email. You'll receive a verification link at the new address.
