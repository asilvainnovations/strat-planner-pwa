Phase 1: Environmental Diagnosis (SWOT)
Guided forms ensure complete, consistent SWOT analysis

Manual SWOT Entry

  1. Navigate to Diagnostics → SWOT Analysis
  2. You'll see four quadrants — Strengths (green, internal positive), Weaknesses (red, internal negative), Opportunities (blue, external positive), Threats (orange, external negative)
  3. Click "+ Add Item" in the appropriate quadrant, enter a clear specific statement, add Evidence and set Impact Level (High / Medium / Low), then click "Save"
Best Practice: Be specific rather than generic. Instead of "Good team," write: "Experienced data science team with 15+ years combined industry experience, validated by a 94% client retention rate in 2024."

AI-Assisted SWOT Generation

  1. Click the "AI Assist" button in the SWOT header
  2. Provide context — describe your organization, market position, current challenges, and paste relevant data (annual reports, survey results, meeting notes)
  3. Select which quadrants to generate and click "Generate SWOT Analysis"
  4. Review AI-generated items: "Add to Plan" to include, "Edit" to modify, or "Discard" to reject
⚠️
Always Review AI Suggestions
AI suggestions are intelligent starting points, not final answers. Always verify accuracy, add organization-specific evidence, and adjust language before finalizing your SWOT.
✦
AI Suggest Button
While viewing any section, click the ✦ AI Suggest button in the top-right of the section to get context-aware recommendations — new SWOT items, strategic options, or KPI targets — based on your current plan data and industry.

Phase 2: Systems Thinking
Visualize non-linear relationships across SWOT variables

Causal Loop Diagrams (CLDs)

CLDs reveal how different strategic factors influence each other through feedback loops, surfacing hidden leverage points that linear analysis misses.

  1. Go to Systems Thinking → Causal Loop Diagram Builder
  2. Add Nodes — double-click the canvas to create a variable, label it (e.g., "Employee Training," "Customer Satisfaction"), and categorize it as a SWOT type (Strength, Weakness, Opportunity, Threat)
  3. Create Links — drag from one node to another. Set polarity: + (positive) means "if A increases, B increases"; − (negative) means "if A increases, B decreases"
  4. Use Auto Layout to organize nodes radially, then drag to customize
  5. Review the Loop Analysis panel — Reinforcing loops (R) amplify change; Balancing loops (B) resist change
  6. Check Leverage Points — variables driving the most loops are your highest-impact intervention targets

Systems Archetypes

Apply pre-built archetype templates to recognize common strategic patterns in your context:

📈
Limits to Growth

A reinforcing process builds results until hitting constraints that slow growth. Intervention: remove or weaken the constraint before it limits progress.

⚖️
Shifting the Burden

Short-term fixes relieve symptoms but undermine fundamental solutions. Intervention: invest in the root-cause solution before symptoms become entrenched.

🔺
Escalation

Two parties reinforce each other's aggressive actions, creating an arms race. Intervention: unilateral de-escalation or negotiated agreement.

🔧
Fixes that Fail

A fix creates delayed side effects that worsen the original problem. Intervention: map delayed consequences before implementing any solution.

Phase 3: Strategy Formulation
TOWS methodology — SO, ST, WO, WT strategic options
Opportunities (External) Threats (External)
Strengths (Internal) SO Strategies (Maxi-Maxi) — Use strengths to seize opportunities ST Strategies (Maxi-Mini) — Use strengths to counter threats
Weaknesses (Internal) WO Strategies (Mini-Maxi) — Overcome weaknesses via opportunities WT Strategies (Mini-Mini) — Minimize weaknesses and avoid threats

Building Your Strategy Matrix

  1. Navigate to Strategy → Strategy Matrix
  2. Click "+ Add Strategy" in the appropriate quadrant and write a clear statement linking specific SWOT elements
  3. Set Priority (High / Medium / Low) and link to specific SWOT items for traceability
  4. Use ✦ AI Generate Strategies — select focus area (e.g., Market Expansion, Digital Transformation) and time horizon, then review and apply AI suggestions

Example SO Strategy

SO: "Leverage our strong brand recognition (S1) to capture government digitalization contracts (O2) through a dedicated public sector business development team by Q3 2025."

Phase 4: Balanced Scorecard
Smart KPI categorization across four perspectives
💰 Financial

How do we look to shareholders? Revenue, cost reduction, ROI, EBITDA margin.

👥 Customer

How do customers see us? Satisfaction (NPS), retention rate, market share, new acquisitions.

⚙️ Internal Process

What must we excel at? Process automation, cycle time reduction, quality metrics, IT modernization.

🌱 Learning & Growth

How can we sustain improvement? Employee satisfaction, training hours, innovation index, capability development.

Creating KPIs

  1. Go to Scorecard → Balanced Scorecard and select a perspective tab
  2. Click "+ Add KPI" and define: KPI Name, Target, Unit (%, ₱, days, score), Weight (0–1.0), Actual value, and Status
  3. Click "Save KPI" — the system tracks variance automatically

KPI Status & Automated Alerts

🟢 On Track — meeting or exceeding target 🟡 At Risk — within 10% of missing target → email alert triggered 🔴 Behind — missing target by >10% → email alert triggered ✅ Complete — target achieved
ℹ️
Automated KPI Alerts
When a KPI is updated to "At Risk" or "Behind," the plan owner automatically receives an email notification with the KPI name, target, actual value, and a direct link to the Scorecard. Configure alert preferences under Settings → Notifications.

Phase 5: Initiatives & Execution
Programs, Activities, and Projects with automated budget totaling
🏗
Initiative Hierarchy
Programs
Large-scale, long-term — e.g., "Digital Transformation Program"
Projects
Discrete deliverables — e.g., "CRM Implementation"
Activities
Specific tasks or events — e.g., "Staff Training Workshop"

Creating Initiatives

  1. Navigate to Execution → Initiatives and click "+ New Initiative"
  2. Complete: Name, Type (Program/Project/Activity), Owner, Budget (₱), Utilized (₱), Progress (0–100%), Status, and Due Date
  3. Link to Strategy — select which strategy(ies) this initiative supports
  4. Click "Save Initiative"

Budget Tracking

The system automatically calculates Total Budget, Total Utilized, Remaining, and Burn Rate from all initiatives — no manual summation required. The stats panel at the top of the Initiatives page updates in real-time as you add or edit initiatives.

Collaboration & Team Management
Real-time presence, comments, permissions, and org management

Sharing a Plan

  1. Open the plan and click the "Share" button (top right)
  2. Enter your colleague's email address
  3. Set permission level: Viewer (read-only — for boards and consultants), Editor (add/edit but not delete plan or manage sharing), or Admin (full control)
  4. Add an optional context message, then click "Send Invitation"
  5. The recipient receives an email notification with a direct link to the plan
ℹ️
Account Required
Recipients must have a Strat Planner Pro account to access shared plans. Registration is free. Email invitations include a registration link for new users.
🟢
Real-Time Collaboration Features
  • Live Presence Indicators: Colored avatars in the top bar show who is currently online and viewing the plan
  • Live Cursors: See where teammates are working within the same section
  • Instant Updates: Changes appear immediately on everyone's screen via WebSocket — no refresh needed
  • Comment Threads: Click the 💬 icon on any SWOT item, KPI, or initiative to start a threaded discussion — tagged members receive email notifications
  • Activity Log: Full audit trail showing who changed what and when, accessible from the Collaboration menu

Organization Management

  1. Go to Profile → Organizations and click "Create Organization"
  2. Enter organization name and type (Corporation, Government Agency, NGO, Academic Institution)
  3. Invite team members via "Invite Members" — set role (Viewer, Editor, or Admin) and expiration (invitations expire in 7 days for security)

Organization vs. Plan Permissions

Org Membership gives access to the organization's template library and shared resources.
Plan Sharing gives specific access to individual strategic plans — a user can be in your org but only have Viewer access to specific plans.

Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions and troubleshooting
General Usage
Can I use Strat Planner Pro on my mobile device?
▾
Yes. The app is fully responsive and installable as a PWA (Progressive Web App) on both Android and iOS. For best results when editing complex Causal Loop Diagrams, use landscape mode on tablets. The app also works offline — changes sync automatically when you reconnect.
Is my data secure?
▾
Yes. All connections use HTTPS/TLS encryption. Passwords are hashed using bcrypt (12-round cost factor). Data is stored in secure databases with automated backups. JWT tokens expire after 7 days and refresh tokens rotate to prevent reuse. Your OpenAI API key is stored locally in your browser — it is never transmitted to or stored on our servers.
What happens if I lose internet connection?
▾
The app operates in offline mode via a Service Worker and IndexedDB local storage. An "⚠ You're offline" banner appears at the bottom of the screen. All changes are saved locally. When you reconnect, a background sync process automatically pushes changes to the server with last-write-wins conflict resolution — or prompts you to resolve conflicts manually.
Can I export my data?
▾
Yes. Go to Plan Settings → Export Data to download your complete plan in JSON format. For human-readable documents, use the Plan Generator to produce print-ready PDF reports. Organization admins can export all organizational data from the Admin Dashboard.
Collaboration
How many people can collaborate on one plan?
▾
There is no hard technical limit. For optimal WebSocket performance, we recommend a maximum of 20 simultaneous editors per plan. Unlimited viewers are supported. For very large teams, consider using the comment-and-discuss workflow to coordinate who edits which section at any given time.
What if two people edit the same item simultaneously?
▾
The system uses "last-write-wins" with a conflict detection layer. If conflicts are detected (e.g., after offline sync), the platform prompts you to choose: keep your local version, accept the server version, or merge both (which unions items without duplicating by ID). Presence indicators showing active cursors help teams avoid editing the same item at the same time.
Technical Issues
The page isn't loading or is showing an error.
▾
Try these steps in order:
  1. Refresh the browser (F5 or Ctrl+R / Cmd+R)
  2. Clear browser cache and cookies for strategy-ai-planner.deploypad.app
  3. Log out and log back in
  4. Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari)
  5. Contact support@deploypad.app if the issue persists — include your browser version and a screenshot
I didn't receive my invitation email.
▾
Check your spam/junk folder first. If not found, verify the email address was typed correctly. Invitations expire after 7 days — ask the sender to resend if yours has expired. Also check that your email provider isn't filtering emails from noreply@asilvainnovations.com — add it to your safe senders list.
How do I reset my password?
▾
On the login page, click "Forgot Password." Enter your registered email address and click Submit. You'll receive a password reset link valid for 1 hour. For security, resetting your password logs you out of all active sessions on all devices. Contact support if you no longer have access to your registered email.
